## Hero

**Headline (H1):** The services era, archived.
**Lede 1:** From 2019 to 2025, Perlara operated as a patient-foundation consulting firm — shepherding parent-initiated studies and N-of-1 protocols, including the work that became *Maggie's Pearl*. ## What changed (2-card grid)

**Kicker:** What changed
**Headline (H2):** From services to full-stack.

**Card 1 · 2.0 · 2019–2025 — Patient-first consulting.**
The 2.0 era was structured as a consulting practice for rare-disease families and foundations. Engagements were discrete: a yeast model, a screen, a published preprint, a handoff to academic clinicians. *Maggie's Pearl* (epalrestat for PMM2-CDG) was the flagship.

**Card 2 · warm · 3.0 · 2025–present — Full-stack drug development.**
Perlara 3.0 carries the same yeast-first, family-anchored ethos but sits one rung deeper in the value chain — owning compounds through IND-enabling studies and trials, funded by the NCATS Direct-to-Phase-II SBIR and a coalition of disease foundations.

### Callout — What carried over

Yeast as the workhorse. PBC as the legal structure. Patients as the platform. Open notebooks as the publishing model. The 2.0 site retired; the 2.0 commitments did not.
