Archive · Perlara 2.0 (2019–2025)

The services era, archived.

From 2019 to 2025, Perlara operated as a patient-foundation consulting firm — shepherding parent-initiated studies and N-of-1 protocols, including the work that became Maggie’s Pearl. With the November 2025 SBIR award, Perlara transitioned into a full-stack drug development company (Perlara 3.0), and the 2.0 site has been retired in favor of what you see today.

We’re preserving the 2.0 site as a snapshot below for historical and citation purposes. For the original 2014–2019 biotech era, see the Perlara 1.0 archive.

What changed

From services to full-stack.

2.0 · 2019–2025

Patient-first consulting.

The 2.0 era was structured as a consulting practice for rare-disease families and foundations. Engagements were discrete: a yeast model, a screen, a published preprint, a handoff to academic clinicians. Maggie’s Pearl (epalrestat for PMM2-CDG) was the flagship.

3.0 · 2025–present

Full-stack drug development.

Perlara 3.0 carries the same yeast-first, family-anchored ethos but sits one rung deeper in the value chain — owning compounds through IND-enabling studies and trials, funded by the NCATS Direct-to-Phase-II SBIR and a coalition of disease foundations.

What carried over

Yeast as the workhorse. PBC as the legal structure. Patients as the platform. Open notebooks as the publishing model. The 2.0 site retired; the 2.0 commitments did not. Read the full arc on the About page →
