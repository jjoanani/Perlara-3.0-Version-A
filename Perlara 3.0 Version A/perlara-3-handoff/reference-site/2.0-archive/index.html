Perlara 2.0 · 2019–2024

Drug repurposing as a service for rare disease families.

From 2019 through 2024, Perlara operated as a drug-repurposing-as-a-service company. We partnered directly with patient families, foundations, and translational centers to run bespoke yeast-first screens for specific genotypes, then validate candidates in patient-derived models.

This era is now closed. Perlara's current work is internal programs on the REMIT platform and the broader IMD pipeline. See our About page for the full arc from 1.0 to 3.0.

What Perlara 2.0 offered

PerlQuest

Family-led drug discovery

Crowdfunded or foundation-sponsored campaigns to build a yeast avatar for a specific disease gene, screen thousands of compounds, and validate hits in patient-derived material.

PerlArk™

Repurposing platform

A library of 24,000+ compounds (ReFRAME, TargetMol, Pharmakon, Microsource Spectrum) and a growing catalog of humanized yeast avatars across mitochondrial, CDG, and neurometabolic disease.

Translation

Patient-derived validation

Hits progressed from yeast into patient fibroblasts, zebrafish, and iPSC-derived organoids. Parent-partnered N-of-1 studies reported back to the originating family.

Case studies

PMM2-CDG

Maggie's Cure

Yeast screen identified epalrestat (an aldose reductase inhibitor) as an elevator of PMM2 enzymatic activity in patient fibroblasts. Preprint published on bioRxiv.

SURF1 Leigh

Parent-partnered screens

10,000+ compounds across three libraries surfaced 20–30 yeast hits; half validated in SURF1 patient fibroblasts. Basis for the current REMIT-CIV program.

PIGW-CDG

OTC combination

A PIGW-CDG family partnership surfaced two over-the-counter nutraceuticals as actionable yeast hits. The combination is in parent-driven use.

Why 2.0 wound down

By late 2024, the service model had generated a rich library of yeast avatars and a durable set of compound leads \u2014 but the economics of per-family campaigns were fundamentally mismatched with the scale of the IMD long tail. In November 2025, a Direct-to-Phase-2 NIH SBIR award for the REMIT platform catalyzed the shift to Perlara 3.0: internal programs, pooled IMD portfolios, and rational drug cocktails aimed at shared pathway defects. See Pipeline for the programs that grew out of these 2.0 engagements.
